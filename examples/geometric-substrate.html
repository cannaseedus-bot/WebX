Geometric Execution Substrate

D12WebX uses geometry as a computation model: tensors live in manifold M (ℝ²/ℝ³), K'uhul controls phase sequencing, SVG-3D stores tensor state, and execution engines (WASM / WebGPU) operate on the geometry.

1. Manifold M — Execution Space

All tensors are embedded in M (ℝ² or ℝ³). Operations happen inside M — not on raw arrays. SVG-3D encodes tensor positions as geometric elements (circle cx/cy = coordinates in M).

4. Phase Cycle (Deterministic Execution)

[K'ayab' phase_cycle]
  # Phase 0: Load tensors from M
  [Yax tensor_A] → [Sek load_from_manifold M]

  # Phase π/4: Apply geometric operators
  [Yax tensor_A] → [Sek ⊗ tensor_B] → [Ch'en tensor_C]

  # Phase π/2: Validate constraints
  [Yax tensor_C] → [Sek ⊜ constraint_set]

  # Phase 3π/4: Commit to M
  [Yax tensor_C] → [Sek commit_to_manifold M]

  # Phase π: Fold complete
  [Sek advance_phase]
[Kumk'u phase_cycle]
